Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

The creators of DriverPass wanted us to create an app that would help new drivers learn the rules of the road and how to drive. They wanted us to create an app that was accessible online, where those customers could buy different training packages.

What did you do particularly well?

I felt pretty good about the diagrams I made. I came into this course a bit unsure about how all of the different UML diagrams worked and fit together. Having created several of those diagrams throughout the course now, I felt that the ones I submitted in my final project were much better than I thought myself capable of.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

That would probably be work I did in the Business Requirements document. I felt like I wasn't as understanding of the meaning of functional and non functional requirements when I submitted that assignment. Looking back, I think I could provide more in depth descriptions of those requirements.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

Well I tried to take everything that was asked for in the interview transcript we had and translate it into the diagrams I made. When a particular action or function was mentioned in the transcript, that was made into a use case in the use case diagram. When a news user type was spoken about, that was a new object in the uml class diagram. The reason for following the user's needs is pretty simple I think. Those are the people paying you. If you don't follow the user's wants and requests, they at best won't hire you again, and at worst they might sue you or take other action.

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I think from now on I will utilize the diagrams I learned in this class. They really help to show you what is going on inside a system. They are also a simple way to show the client what it is you are working on, in a not quite so technical way.
